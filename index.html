Franchise Identity & Overview

The Washington Wizards are an American professional basketball team based in Washington, D.C. The Wizards compete in the National Basketball Association (NBA) as a member of the league's Eastern Conference Southeast Division. The team plays its home games in the Capital One Arena, located in the Chinatown neighborhood of Washington, D.C.

Established in 1961 as the Chicago Packers, the franchise underwent several name changes and relocations—becoming the Zephyrs, moving to Baltimore to become the Bullets, and eventually heading to the capital region. Renamed the Wizards in 1997 to project a fresh modern aesthetic, the franchise has a rich tapestry of historic draft selections, superstar runs, and passionate fan cultures.

Historical Timelines & Eras

1977-1978 — The Championship Glory

Led by Hall of Famers Elvin Hayes and Wes Unseld, the team captured its first and only NBA Championship by defeating the Seattle SuperSonics in an epic 7-game series.

2001-2003 — The Michael Jordan Era

Basketball legend Michael Jordan came out of retirement for a historic final act, playing his last career seasons in Washington and selling out arenas worldwide.

2010-2019 — The John Wall Flash Era

Drafted #1 overall in 2010, John Wall brought explosive speed and electric playmaking back to D.C., launching multiple deep Eastern Conference playoff iterations.
